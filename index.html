Rusty Kuntz
Outfielder · Detroit Tigers · 1979–1985

Russell Jay Kuntz — "Rusty" — is the greatest name in baseball history, and he backed it up. A journeyman outfielder who played for five teams across seven seasons, Kuntz saved his finest moment for the biggest stage.

In Game 5 of the 1984 World Series, with the Detroit Tigers on the verge of the championship, Rusty Kuntz drove in the game-winning RBI. Detroit won it all, 4–1. The name. The moment. The legend.

After his playing days, Kuntz became one of baseball's most respected coaches. Kansas City manager Ned Yost called him "the best first base coach in baseball."
